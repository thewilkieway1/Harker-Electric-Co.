Common Questions

ELECTRICAL SERVICES FAQ.

Do I need a permit for an electrical panel upgrade in Port St. Lucie?

Yes — all electrical panel upgrades in Port St. Lucie require a permit from St. Lucie County. Voltex Electric pulls all necessary permits on your behalf as part of every panel upgrade job. We also schedule and pass the final inspection so you have documentation that the work was done to code.

How long does a whole home rewire take?

Most whole home rewires in Port St. Lucie take between 3 and 7 days depending on the size and age of the home. We work efficiently to minimize disruption and clean up completely at the end of every shift. Older homes with difficult access may take longer, and we'll give you an accurate timeline during your free estimate.

What is a residential electrical services inspection and do I need one?

An electrical inspection involves a licensed electrician reviewing your home's panel, wiring, outlets, grounding, and smoke detector placement for safety and code compliance. We recommend one if you're buying a home, experiencing recurring electrical problems, or if your home is over 25 years old. Inspections start at $149.

Can you install a Level 2 EV charger in a condo or apartment?

In some cases, yes — it depends on the building's electrical infrastructure and HOA rules. We've successfully installed dedicated charging circuits in garages of condos and townhomes throughout Port St. Lucie. We assess feasibility during the estimate at no charge.

Do you serve areas outside of Port St. Lucie?

Yes — Voltex Electric serves all of St. Lucie County, Indian River County, and Martin County, including Fort Pierce, Vero Beach, Stuart, Palm City, and Jensen Beach. Call to confirm service availability for your specific address.
